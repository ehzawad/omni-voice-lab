The Laws of the Game are maintained by the International Football Association Board (IFAB), not FIFA alone. There are 17 Laws of the Game.

A standard football match lasts 90 minutes, split into two halves of 45 minutes each, with a half-time interval not exceeding 15 minutes.

The offside rule (Law 11): a player is in an offside position if any part of the head, body, or feet is in the opponents' half and nearer to the opponents' goal line than both the ball and the second-last opponent. Being in an offside position is not an offence in itself.

A player is penalised for offside only if, at the moment the ball is played by a teammate, they are involved in active play by interfering with play, interfering with an opponent, or gaining an advantage.

A direct free kick is awarded for offences like kicking, tripping, charging, or handling the ball. A goal can be scored directly from a direct free kick. An indirect free kick requires the ball to touch another player before a goal counts.

A penalty kick is awarded when a direct-free-kick offence is committed by a player inside their own penalty area. The penalty spot is 11 metres (12 yards) from the goal line.

Yellow card = caution; red card = sending-off. Two yellow cards in one match equal a red card, and the player must leave the field and cannot be replaced.

The FIFA World Cup is held every four years. The first World Cup was held in 1930 in Uruguay, which Uruguay won.

Brazil has won the FIFA Men's World Cup a record five times: 1958, 1962, 1970, 1994, and 2002.

Germany and Italy have each won the Men's World Cup four times. Argentina has won three times: 1978, 1986, and 2022.

The 2022 FIFA World Cup was held in Qatar and won by Argentina, who beat France on penalties after a 3-3 draw in the final. Lionel Messi won the Golden Ball.

The 2018 FIFA World Cup was held in Russia and won by France, who beat Croatia 4-2 in the final.

Miroslav Klose of Germany is the all-time top scorer in FIFA World Cup history with 16 goals.

The Ballon d'Or is awarded annually to the best footballer; it is organised by France Football, not FIFA. Lionel Messi has won it a record number of times.

FIFA's own annual award for the best player is now called The Best FIFA Men's Player (and Women's Player), separate from the Ballon d'Or.

VAR (Video Assistant Referee) reviews four match-changing situations: goals, penalty decisions, direct red-card incidents, and mistaken identity.

A football team fields 11 players, one of whom is the goalkeeper. A match cannot continue if a team has fewer than seven players.

The FIFA Women's World Cup was first held in 1991. The United States women's national team has won it a record four times.

The 2023 FIFA Women's World Cup was co-hosted by Australia and New Zealand and won by Spain, who beat England 1-0 in the final.

A hat-trick is when a single player scores three goals in one match.

The Golden Boot (Top Scorer award) goes to the player who scores the most goals in a World Cup tournament.

Substitutions: in most competitions teams may now make up to five substitutions during a match, following a temporary rule introduced in 2020 that became permanent.

The penalty area (18-yard box) measures 16.5 metres from the goal line. The goal is 7.32 metres wide and 2.44 metres high.

If a knockout match is level after 90 minutes, it goes to 30 minutes of extra time, and if still level, a penalty shootout decides the winner.

FIFA (Fédération Internationale de Football Association) was founded in 1904 and is headquartered in Zurich, Switzerland. It governs international football worldwide.

The continental confederations under FIFA are UEFA (Europe), CONMEBOL (South America), CONCACAF (North/Central America & Caribbean), CAF (Africa), AFC (Asia), and OFC (Oceania).
